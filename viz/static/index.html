Software that has to keep itself alive

Why a statue isn't alive, and how each death taught the next organism.

Almost every program is a statue: you carve it once and it just sits there. We're building software more like a living cell — it must constantly rebuild its own parts, or it falls apart. Being alive isn't a thing you are, it's a thing you keep doing.

So we made a sealed world where anything not constantly rewritten dissolves — like writing in sand while the tide washes over it. A normal program dies instantly; our organisms redraw their bodies faster than the tide erases them.
